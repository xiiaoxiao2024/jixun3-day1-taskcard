1Reading

Listen to the story, then read along. Record yourself reading when you're ready.
先听故事，再跟读。准备好了就录音读一遍。

Our class began to prepare for the bake sale.

The money from the sale would pay for our class trip.

We wanted everything to be perfect for the big event.

We started to gather all the trays onto the table.

A big balloon was tied to the front of the table.

Then a strange accident happened near the dessert table.

Two students began to mix up the labels by mistake.

One tray said "Chocolate," but it was actually vanilla.

This mix-up began to confuse everyone at the table.

The wrong labels caused a lot of confusion.

"This is a huge mistake," said one boy.

He looked a little upset and disappointed about the mix-up.

Suddenly, someone tried to hurry and fix the trays.

A tray started to fall before anyone could stop it!

A parent volunteer caught the tray just in time.

The whole crowd let out a loud cheer.

The audience laughed at the noise and the chaos.

It felt like a funny trick, not a real problem.

One boy admitted that he felt a little foolish.

He tried not to regret it too much.

"Let's not fight about it," said the teacher.

Everyone agreed it wasn't worth getting mad over.

For a moment, nobody knew what to do.

The whole event felt a little chaotic and strange.

Then someone had a good idea to fix it.

They decided to shout out the correct names.

They walked past each tray, calling out its flavor.

It worked, and everyone began to relax a little.

The trays still looked a bit strange and mixed up.

A teacher stepped onto the small stage by the door.

A student agreed to perform a quick, funny joke first.

She wanted to announce the surprise plan to everyone.

She explained that every dessert would get a big sign.

That way, nobody would forget the flavor again.

The class began to express how funny it all was.

Even the shy students started to smile and laugh.

One girl said it felt like being in a contest.

It was a test of who could react the fastest.

Her reaction made everyone laugh again and again.

There was almost a tear in every person's eye.

When the sale finally began, it went very well.

It succeeded better than anyone had expected.

People came from every class to see the bake sale.

It felt like the whole school was there in public.

Soon a huge crowd had gathered around the table.

A few visitors seemed afraid to guess wrong.

So they asked questions before choosing a dessert.

By the end of the day, it was time to report.

The class told the principal exactly what had happened.

She smiled and called it a special memory.

She said it was not a terrible mistake at all.

Everyone felt proud that they fixed the mix-up together.

Nobody stayed upset about it for very long.

It wasn't a perfect start to the day.

But in the end, it became an amazing, funny story.

The whole school wanted to congratulate the class.

They had turned an awful moment into a happy one.
