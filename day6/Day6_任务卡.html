1Reading

Listen to the story, then read along. Record yourself reading when you're ready.
先听故事，再跟读。准备好了就录音读一遍。

Mia and Leo have played on the same soccer team for two years.

They are both important players.

But they are very different from each other.

Mia is loud and active on the field.

Leo, on the other hand, is much quieter and calmer.

Before every game, Mia talks to everyone with joy.

She laughs and shouts, and she never feels tired.

Leo likes to sit alone before a game.

He usually just thinks quietly about the plan.

Some people think Leo is boring.

But he is actually very wise about strategy.

He is not interested in being the loudest person on the team.

Mia is quick and strong, not weak at all.

She loves a hard challenge on the field.

Leo is not as fast, and he is quite thin.

He runs at a slow, steady speed, never too fast.

Still, his skill at passing the ball is excellent.

Unlike Mia, Leo never gets too excited about a goal.

Both players work hard, but in very different ways.

Mia practices for a whole hour every single day.

She never feels bored, even when it is difficult.

Leo prefers a simple, short practice.

After practice, he enjoys plenty of rest.

One time, Mia felt sick and had to rest for a week.

Leo visited her every day so she would not feel alone.

Their coach says this difference is a good thing.

"A team needs different skills, not just one favorite way," she says.

Both of them shared a dream: to win the city championship.

One day, the team faced a difficult challenge.

The other team was rich with famous, talented players.

Mia felt hungry to win against them.

She was ready to play a fast, loud game.

Leo felt a little nervous at first.

But he stayed calm and made a clear plan.

"I know it sounds crazy, but let's just play our own game," he said.

"Let's continue with our own style," Leo said quietly.

Mia agreed, even though she would rather play faster.

During the game, Mia's speed caused trouble for the other team.

One defender pushed Mia, but it did not really hurt.

Meanwhile, Leo's passes were soft and always clear.

The two styles were unique, but they worked well together.

By the second half, both players felt the same real hope.

The score was close, and it stayed close until the end.

Mia made a quick run down the field.

Leo saw the perfect moment and made his pass.

His pass was simple, but her finish was strong.

The whole team celebrated with loud, happy joy.

The reporter arrived a little late, but the team was fine with waiting.

He had once written an ugly story about the team, but not this time.

After the game, a reporter asked them about their secret.

Mia laughed and said, "We are not similar at all."

"And that is exactly the point," she added.

Mia said she used to hate playing with someone so different.

Now she is free of that feeling completely.

Leo smiled, which was rather rare for him.

"We are fair to each other," he said simply.

Their coach said this was the most important opinion of all.

She said that being different does not mean being unfair.

It was never a stupid question to ask why they got along.

"Thank you for asking such a good question," she told the reporter.

In fact, two different players can still make an excellent team.

Mia is still the loud, quick one.

Leo is still the quiet, wise one.

But their friendship is real, not just a rule.

Some fans find Leo's calm style a little boring.

They compare it to Mia's busy, active energy.

Other fans think Mia's loud style is a bit too much.

They compare it to Leo's simple, easy style.

Either opinion misses the real point of the story.

It is not about which style is better.

It is not even about who is tall or short.

It is about two people who still respect each other.

Their coach often tells new players about this unique friendship.

"You don't have to be similar to be a great team," she explains.

"You just have to be fair, honest, and ready to grow."

By the end of the season, the whole team understood this idea.

Being different was never a problem for Mia and Leo.

It was actually their biggest strength as a team.

The team was not rich or poor; it was simply a good team.

And their friendship became a favorite topic at every practice after that.
