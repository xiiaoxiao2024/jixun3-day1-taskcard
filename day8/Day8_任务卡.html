1Reading

Listen to the story, then read along. Record yourself reading when you're ready.
先听故事，再跟读。准备好了就录音读一遍。

Ivy wanted this camping vacation to be perfect.

Her family planned to drive to a quiet spot near the hills.

There, they could swim, fish, and sing songs around a small fire.

Ivy's favorite hobby was collecting old items from her grandmother's house, and this time she wanted to bring something special.

It was an old ring that had belonged to her grandmother.

It was not a new ring, but Ivy loved it more than anything new.

She wanted to keep it safe on the whole trip.

So she tucked it deep inside her bag.

When the family arrived at the campsite, the sky was bright and sunny.

Only a few white clouds sat far away.

Ben, Ivy's older brother, helped hang the tent between two trees.

It stood near a small bridge over a stream.

He tied the rope tight so it would not shake in the wind.

Their friend Cole came along too, carrying a flag to mark their camping spot.

By noon, the sun felt warm, and everyone was thirsty after the long drive.

So they filled their water bottles at the spot near the bridge.

That afternoon, they had a picnic near the water.

Ivy put on her favorite cap to keep the sun off her face.

After lunch, everyone took a quick bath in the cool stream.

They used soap and a towel to get clean again.

Cole took a photograph of the three friends standing by the bridge.

They were laughing and pointing at a fish jumping out of the water.

Ivy liked to count the fish she saw, and she counted seven before dinner.

But that evening, dark clouds rolled in, and the sky grew cloudy and windy.

Rain started to fall, and everyone rushed to keep their things dry inside the tent.

In the rush, Ivy asked Cole to hold her bag for just a moment.

That is when her precious ring rolled out into the wet grass.

Nobody noticed a thing.

By the time the rain stopped, it was already dark outside.

The next morning, Ivy reached into her bag to check on her ring.

It was gone.

She began to cry.

"Where is it?" she asked, her voice shaking.

Ben remembered that Cole had been holding the bag the night before.

So he asked Cole directly, "Did you take Ivy's ring?" Cole looked surprised and a little hurt.

"I never touched it," he said, but Ben was not sure he believed him.

A cold difference grew between the two friends.

Nobody wanted to talk about it.

Ivy did not want to give up, so she wanted to try one more search before breakfast was over.

The three of them searched every spot near the tent, the bridge, and the hill nearby.

They looked under the wet grass and inside every bag.

They even checked the old fish basket, but they could not find the ring anywhere.

Then Ben remembered something.

The night before, right after the rain, he had seen a bright flash of light near the tree by the bridge.

Maybe it was just luck, but he decided to go look again.

Under the tree, half buried in the mud, he found the ring at last.

Ben ran back to return the ring to Ivy, who could not stop smiling.

"I'm sorry I doubted you," Ben said to Cole.

"It's not true that you took it.

The fact is, it just fell out of the bag in the rain." Cole listened and nodded.

"I understand why you were upset," he said.

"But next time, let's agree to talk about it first, before we blame anyone." Ben decided to explain the whole story again, slower this time.

So everyone understood exactly what had happened.

After that, the three friends made peace and promised to be more careful with important things.

Ivy wanted to learn from what happened.

So she promised to change her habit of packing loose items.

She also promised to keep the ring somewhere safer next time.

That night, the sky was finally clear again, and they each made a wish on the first star they saw.

"I wonder if next year's trip will be just as exciting," Cole said with a grin.

In the end, Ivy decided this camping vacation was still her favorite one yet, ring and all.

Cole's photograph became her favorite picture from the whole trip, and she kept the little item close to her heart from then on.

Every time she saw it, she remembered the friends who helped her find it.

It was a small ring, but it now carried a whole story.

It was one she would want to retell for years to come.
