1Reading

Listen to the story, then read along. Record yourself reading when you're ready.

Mia was new at school. She felt shy.

At lunch, she looked for a seat.

She saw a group of kids. They were laughing.

"Can I sit here?" Mia asked.

No one answered right away, and Mia felt alone.

Then a girl named Ruby looked up.

"Sorry! Yes, sit here," Ruby said kindly.

Mia sat down. Ruby was nice and friendly.

"We have a secret club," Ruby said. "Want to hear about it?"

Mia smiled. She felt included now.

A boy named Theo was in Ruby's group too.

He shared his snack with Mia.

"We made a promise," Theo said. "Only good friends can join."

"What do I do?" Mia asked.

"Just be a good friend," Ruby said, and she smiled.

Mia felt comfortable and proud.

The next day, the class got a group project.

"Find a group of four," the teacher said.

Mia was in a group with Ruby, Theo, and a new student named Farah.

"Who wants to do what?" Ruby asked.

"I can draw," Theo said quickly.

Farah wanted to present, but she felt shy about saying it.

"I could present," Farah said softly.

Mia felt happy for her, and she said, "That's great!"

The group worked hard, but they had a small problem.

"This isn't fair," Theo said. "I have too much to do."

"Let's be honest about our work," Ruby said.

They talked, and they helped each other.

Farah felt confident now, and she practiced her lines with Mia.

By Friday, their poster was done, and every member helped.

"Great teamwork," the teacher said, and she congratulated the whole group.

Mia felt proud. She had good friends, and she trusted them.
